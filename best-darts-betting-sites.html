    • What are some popular darts betting markets?

    Betting on darts is incredibly popular in the UK. Many bookmakers offer a wide range of betting markets. Here are some of the most common markets you’ll find:

    • Outright tournament winner bet - This one is self-explanatory. Essentially, you’ll have to predict which players will win a certain competition.
    • Match win bet - With this bet, you’re only wagering on the winner of a specific match. This is one of the most common bets in darts.
    • Set win bet - Darts bookmakers also allow you to bet on specific sets. The final match result doesn’t count towards this bet.
    • Nine-darter bet - In simple terms, a nine-darter is a perfect game. If you place this bet, your selected player has to score 501 points with exactly nine darts.
    • Most 180s - The maximum points you can hit with three darts is 180. To win this bet, you’ll have to guess which of the opposing players gets the most 180s during the match.
    • Correct score - To win a Correct Score bet, you’ll have to correctly predict how many sets each player will win. 
    • Handicap betting - Handicap bets give one player an advantage or a disadvantage. To win your bet, your chosen player must overcome the disadvantage or stay within the advantage.
    • King of the Oche - King of the Oche combines several bets into one – Match Winner, Most 180s, and the Highest Checkout. The latter requires having the highest-value final dart.

    What are the odds on a nine-darter?

    The odds of hitting a nine-darter on the dartboard are incredibly slim. Since 1996, only 437 nine-dart finishes have been recorded. 

    What are some popular darts tournaments to bet on?

    Darts enthusiasts will be eager to know there are many tournaments throughout the year to bet on. Check out the most popular competitions:

    World Darts Championship – December 

    The World Darts Championship takes place every year between December and January. It has one of the largest prize pools across the sport – £2,500,000.

    Darts Premier League – February

    The Darts Premier League is a weekly competition that takes place yearly, from February to May. A full season features 17 events.

    UK Open Darts – March

    The UK Open is a darts competition held by the Professional Darts Corporation (PDC). It usually takes place in March, with a prize pool of £600,000.

    World Darts Matchplay – July

    You can watch the World Darts Matchplay in July. It’s one of the most competitive events, with a prize pool of £450,000.

    Grand Slam of Darts – November

    The Grand Slam of Darts is also organised by PDC. This competition takes place in November.

    World Grand Prix – October 

    The World Grand Prix is a darts tournament held in October. Players will compete to win from a pot worth £600,000.
