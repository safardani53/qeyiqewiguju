8 Best Leg Press Machines 2020 | The Sun UK

Publish date: 2024-06-24

WHILE there's plenty of home gym kit for developing the upper body, working the legs can be trickier.

Which is why we've rounded up the best leg press machines - these will get your quads, hamstrings, glutes and calves going.

Squats can help develop similar muscles to leg presses, but won't isolate muscle groups as effectively - or prevent you from throwing out your back.

With a leg press machine, your back is supported (either seated or lying down), and you can work several lower body muscle groups with small movements.

You can buy separate leg press machines for the home, or spend a bit more money upfront and invest in a home gym that has an integrated leg press.

It's all a question of space and how much you're prepared to spend - and use the machine in question.

One way to work the legs without giving up a ton of space and cash is to get a leg curl machine. This primarily works the hamstrings.
