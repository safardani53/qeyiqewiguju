Marcella Arguello: Bitch, Grow Up! Is Set To Released On HBO

Publish date: 2024-05-12

Marcella Arguello’s next comedy special, Marcella Arguello: Bitch, Grow Up!, will premiere on HBO Max on February 10, 2023. Arguello, one of Los Angeles’ most exciting Latinx comedians, will present a night full of relatable humorous anecdotes in her trademark style in her new stand-up comedy spectacular. Marcella Arguello: Bitch, Grow Up! was initially scheduled to premiere on HBO Latino on February 10, 2023, before moving to HBO Max for worldwide viewers.

This will be a part of HBO’s popular Entre Nos series, which has seen other notable comedians such as Gina Brillon, Chris Estrada, and Shayla Rivera accomplish great things. Marcella Arguello will be a member of this group when her stand-up special becomes one of HBO Max’s staples. Earlier this month, HBO Max released a teaser for the special, which provides a taste of what to anticipate from the shameless comic.

More on Marcella Arguello: Grow Up, Bitch!

The Marcella Arguello comedy special promises to be as entertaining and accessible as any of her previous efforts. The Warner Brothers website has provided a full summary of what to anticipate from the comedy special. Arguello will apparently address a variety of current issues, ranging from post-pandemic hookups to middle school D.A.R.E. essays. The performance is described as follows on the Warner Brothers website for Marcella Arguello: Bitch, Grow Up! :

“In BITCH, GROW UP!, Arguello combines her larger-than-life stage presence with a brutally honest take on post-pandemic hookups and why broke men are better in bed, as well as an exploration of how “Beauty and the Beast” triggered an epiphany and a self-reflection on her middle school D.A.R.E. essay. Marcella Arguello makes her solo debut with this delightfully relevant stand-up comedy show filmed at Brooklyn’s Roulette Intermedium.”

The Warner Brothers website also highlights how this special will join the large line of Latino comedy specials currently available on HBO Max. These specials include Ian Lara’s Romantic Comedy (2022), Jesus Sepulveda’s Mr. Tough Life (2022), Frankie Quiones’ Superhomies (2021), Nick Guerra’s Love Me at My Worst (2020), Shayla Rivera’s It’s Not Rocket Science (2020), Jerry Garcia’s It’s Not My Weekend (2019), Gina Brillon’s Easily Offended (2019), Erik Rivera’s Super White (2019), and Orlando (2018).

Who is Marcella Arguello?

Marcella Arguello is a comedian from Los Angeles, California. She grew raised in Modesto, California, and was involved in her school’s theater. She was always the class clown, and she excelled at impressions from an early age, according to her. She then spent three years at California State University, Stanislaus, and San Francisco State University. She had intended to become an elementary school teacher before changing her mind and relocating to Los Angeles to pursue a career in comedy.

In her early days, she routinely performed at Improv comedy clubs around the United States, as well as The Punch Line comedy clubs in Northern California. Arguello quickly rose to prominence for her broad range of themes, fearless delivery, and self-deprecating humor.

Marcella Arguello: Bitch, Grow UpWarner !’s Brothers website explains her humorous approach as follows:

“She happily covers any and every issue that tickles her, including dating small people since she doesn’t have any other alternatives at 6’2″, being mistaken for a man, her Latinx identity, her perspective on race and gender, and outrageous anecdotes and situations she’s gotten herself into and out of.”

Marcella Arguello: Bitch, Grow Up! will premiere on HBO Max on February 10, 2023.
