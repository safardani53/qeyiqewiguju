Disney Moana Halloween costume called RACIST, sparking furious Good Morning Britain debate

Publish date: 2024-06-03

A FURIOUS debate broke out on Good Morning Britain today after one guest claimed wearing Disney Moana costumes was racist.

Commentator Radhika Sanghani’s views were branded “certifiably insane” by DJ Julia Hartley-Brewer in a fiery clash.

The row was sparked after a New York mum banned her daughter from dressing up as the Polynesian cartoon character for Halloween, claiming to do so would be “cultural appropriation”.

And this morning Sanghani backed her, saying: “Moana is based on Polynesian cultures in the south pacific.

“These are cultures that have faced oppression and discrimination.

“They’ve had their lands taken away from them and today there is still racial prejudice and discrimination against these people.

“So to dress up in their native dress and use it as a costume that’s just mockery. It’s offensive and disrespectful.”

Hartley-Brewer replied: “This is certifiably insane. It would be funny if it wasn’t so dangerous.

“If my daughter dresses up as Moana that wouldn’t be her mocking or in any way being disrespectful to Polynesian culture. It would be her dressing up as a brilliant, strong, female role model from a film.”

Radhika countered: “I wish we could live in an ideal world where colour and race wasn’t an issue but we don’t. But it is important that at some point children become aware of this.”

Tackling the issue of cultural appropriation Hartley-Brewer sparked a series of testy exchanges, saying: “Cultural appropriation implies in some way theft.

"As if in some way dressing up as a character from a film is stealing someone’s culture or disrespecting it."

Sanghani shot back: “It is disrespecting it. Julia you’re not from those cultures."

Julia countered: “Oh I’ve made the terrible mistake of being white therefore I’m not allowed an opinion”, before Sanghani added: “No . It’s just about being empathetic and trying to put yourself in these people’s shoes and thinking how would you feel if those costumes were mocked.”

It comes as mum Sachi Feris from Brooklyn, New York also urged parents not to allow their kids to wear costumes of Frozen’s Princess Elsa.

She wrote: “There is one thing I don’t like about the character of Elsa. I feel like because Elsa is a White princess, and we see so many white princesses, her character sends the message that you have to be a certain way to be “beautiful” or to be a 'princess'."
