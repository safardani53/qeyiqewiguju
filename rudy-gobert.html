Amateur Career

Gobert began playing basketball for the JSC St-Quentin club in 2003 before joining the Saint-Quentin BB club later that year.

The athlete joined Cholet Basket’s cadet categories training center in 2007, and 2010, he represented France at the FIBA Europe Under-18 Championship, where he was named the team’s best scorer and rebounder.

Gobert mainly played for the Cholet Basket junior team from 2009 to 2011, with only one game for the senior team in 2010–11, when he had 6 points, five rebounds, one steal, and one block in 13 minutes against Pau-Lacq-Orthez on May 10, 2011.

During 29 games for Cholet in 2011–12, he averaged 4.2 points and 3.6 rebounds per game. He averaged 5.4 rebounds and 8.4 points per game in 27 games the following season, 2012–13.

Professional Career

Rudy Gobert announced the 2013 NBA Draft after an outstanding amateur career and was selected as the 27th overall selection by the NBA’s Denver Nuggets on June 27, 2013.

He was traded to the Utah Jazz on draft night and played in the 2013 NBA Summer League.

Rudy improved his game as the season progressed, finishing third in the NBA’s Best Player of the 2014-2015 Season award.

He set a career-high with 22 points in a 112-105 win over the Phoenix Suns the following year. Another highlight of his career came in 2017 when he had 27 points and 25 rebounds in a 112-107 victory over the Dallas Mavericks.

In March 2017, he had another career-high with 35 points and 13 rebounds in a 108-101 win over the New York Knicks.

Rudy won the NBA Defensive Player of the Year award for the third time in four years on June 9, 2021, making him the fourth player in NBA history to win three or more times.

His win marked the first time in NBA history that an international player has won awards for four consecutive defensive players of the year.

In a Game 2 second-round win over the Los Angeles Clippers on June 11, Gobert set a playoff career-high with 20 rebounds.

Presence In Olympics 

Head coach Vincent Collet enlisted Gobert’s assistance in preparing the senior French national team for the 2012 Summer Olympics.

Rudy Gobert was only 23 years old when he traveled to Rio de Janeiro in 2016. He’d just finished his second entire NBA season with the Utah Jazz, averaging less than ten points per game and less than eight defensive rebounds.

Gobert appeared in five games for France, averaging 5.7 points, 7.2 rebounds, and 1.8 blocks per appearance.

He also played in the 2019 FIBA World Cup for France, where he averaged 9.1 rebounds, 10.1 points, 1.8 assists, 0.6 steals, and 1.9 blocks per game.

Gobert led France to a silver medal at the 2020 Olympic Games in Tokyo, starting all games averaging 9.3 rebounds and 12.2 points.

Rudy Gobert: Early Life & Family

Gobert was born to Rudy Bourgarel and Corrine Gobert in Saint-Quentin.

When Rudy was about three years old, his parents divorced, and his father moved to Guadeloupe. 

Gobert stayed in Saint-Quentin with his mother, Corinne Gobert, where he faced challenges as a mixed-race child in France.

According to an NBA profile of Gobert, his mother, Corinne, worked as a hairdresser throughout his childhood while raising her three children as a single mother.

Since he was a child, Rudy Gobert, a Utah Jazz center, visited Guadeloupe, a French archipelago in the Caribbean.

Rudy Bourgarel, his father, lives there and keeps a close eye on his son’s career after his own NBA dreams were dashed years ago.

Gobert claims that his mother used to save enough money every three years to buy the family plane tickets to Guadeloupe to see his father and other relatives.

Comes From A Basketball Family

Rudy Bourgarel, Rudy Gobert’s father, played at Marist College from 1985 to 1988.

Rik Smits, the future No. 2 overall pick in the 1988 draft who spent his 12-year career with the Indiana Pacers, was Bourgarel’s teammate there.

In 1987-88, the 7-foot Bourgarel became a starter, averaging 10.7 points, 6.8 rebounds, and 1.5 blocks per game while playing alongside Smits.

Bourgarel, on the other hand, was never drafted and spent a decade playing professional basketball in Europe.

Gobert claimed that his father, who made a cameo appearance in “Coming To America,” never pushed him to play basketball.

He also expressed his father’s pride in him playing in the NBA, a dream he had but never realized.

As sports analysts continue to point out, Rudy and his father have excellent physical characteristics, including good size, strength, length, and the ability to run the ground.

Rudy Gobert: Nickname

Gobert is known for his shot-blocking ability. But he’s perhaps best known for his NBA nickname, “Stifle Tower,” which he earned just a few weeks into his career.

Jody Genessy of the Deseret News coined the moniker in July of 2013, just two weeks after he was drafted.

Although he didn’t like the moniker at first (according to a Rolling Stone interview from 2014), he warmed up to it after a year.

In 2015, Gobert told NBA.com, “There are a lot of nicknames that are pretty cool.” “‘Stifle Tower’ is one of my favorites. ‘Gobzilla’ is one of my favorites.”
