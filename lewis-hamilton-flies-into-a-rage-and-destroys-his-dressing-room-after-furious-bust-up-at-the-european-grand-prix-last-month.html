Lewis Hamilton flies into a rage and destroys dressing room after furious bust-up at European Grand

Publish date: 2024-08-16

LEWIS HAMILTON exploded in a rage and "destroyed" his dressing room at last month's European Grand Prix.

The details of the astonishing meltdown come from Mercedes chairman, Niki Lauda, who says the Brit will be forced to pay for the damage he caused in Baku.

In an explosive TV interview Lauda also claims Hamilton LIED when he told a Sunday newspaper that his relationship with rival Nico Rosberg was improving.

Hamilton was so livid after he crashed out during qualifying for the European GP in Azerbaijan, Lauda says he took his frustration out in his private room in the team's motor-home.

He said: "He did it because he had crashed. He'll have to pay for that [the destroyed room]. You can count on that.

"He told me I couldn't come in because he was going to destroy everything. This is how it was."

Hamilton had to settle for fifth place in Baku while Rosberg went on to win the race.

Yet afterwards, the defending world champion claimed the duo had buried the hatchet and were privately good friends.

Yet Lauda says Hamilton is making the story up and claims that the two's relationship gets WORSE the longer Rosberg leads the championship.

He added: "Lewis lied about that, simple as that. He just said something. He wanted to be the softener in order to have his peace last weekend.

"He does what he can. The fight gets hotter the longer Nico is in front."

Lauda's amazing revelations will heighten the tensions between the two ahead of this weekend's British GP.

SunSport revealed at the European GP how the two were at loggerheads after clashing in the drivers' briefing when discussing the safety of the pit-lane.

And they were furious with each other after last weekend's Austrian GP when Rosberg crashed into Hamilton on the final lap.

The German, who was in the lead, refused to take the blame and claims Hamilton was in the wrong.

However, race stewards sided with the Brit, who went on to win the race, and slapped Rosberg with a ten-second penalty and a reprimand.

Unhappy Mercedes chiefs have now launched their own investigation into the accident and will speak to both drivers separately on Wednesday.
