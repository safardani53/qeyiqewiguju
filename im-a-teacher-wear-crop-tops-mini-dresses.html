I'm a teacher but still wear crop tops and mini dresses - it doesn't make me any worse at my job

Publish date: 2024-07-28

A WOMAN has shared how she won’t let anything stop her wearing crop tops and mini dresses – even her job as a teacher. 

Social media user Sheena Lynn shared a video on her TikTok profile that hit back at critics, as she explained that the outfits she wears doesn’t affect her ability in the classroom. 

Sheena, whose TikTok profile is @exceptionallyexcited, shared a reel of her twirling for the camera.

In it, the blonde teacher – who describes herself as “a millennial living in a Tik Tok world” – is wearing a pearled cream crop top.

She teamed this with a white ra-ra style mini skirt and silver sparkly heeled boots, and strutted her stuff as she turned to walk away from the camera. 

As she posed for her 26k followers, she wrote that in spite of her career, she should be able to wear what she wants when she wants.

Addressing people who think teachers should dress conservatively at all times, she typed: “Yes, I’m a teacher. 

“But I’m still an adult that likes to wear crop tops and mini dresses, have the occasional adult beverage and leaves work at work.”

She then added: “Doesn’t mean I do my job any less.” 

And it wasn’t long before her opinion got her social media followers and more talking.

The video racked up 26.2k likes as it went viral on the social media platform. 

Countless users also inundated the post with their opinion in the comment section. 

One teacher TikToker wrote: “THIS. Teachers are people too!! 

“You’d be hard-pressed to find another profession as heavily scrutinised as ours. You look fabulous.”

Another teacher was also in agreement, as they waded in on the debate.

They wrote: “I mean I crowd surfed last night at a metal concert! 

“We need to let off steam like everyone else!!!”

While a third stated: “Thiiiiisssssss. I am NOT a professional 24/7. Does not make me less capable!”

And it seems the teacher had plenty of parents on her side too, as they also joined in the conversation.

One said: “As a parent of two school aged children WE LOVE TO SEE IT!!!!”

Another person added: “I don’t care what my kids’ teachers do outside business hours as long as it doesn’t affect my kids!!”
