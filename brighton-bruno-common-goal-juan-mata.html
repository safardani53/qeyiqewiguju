Brighton cult hero Bruno becomes latest Premier League star to donate wages to charity

Publish date: 2024-10-04

BRIGHTON'S bearded cult hero Bruno has signed up to Juan Mata's Common Goal campaign.

The defender and Seagulls captain will now donate one per cent of his wages to charity to help the poor and underprivileged around the world.

Brighton star Bruno signs up with Juan Mata's Common Goal campaign

Talking about why he has signed up to the initiative, the 37-year-old said: "Last season I was fortunate enough to be part of a Brighton team that was promoted to the Premier League for the first time in our history.

"It was a special moment that united our entire community and a powerful reminder of football's capacity to create magic in our lives.

"What I like most about Common Goal is that it taps into this same magic to change the world for good. It's about uniting all of us, the global football community, behind a shared commitment to give back.

"Over the past few years I think we've come to understand that, as players, we have a great opportunity to make a meaningful difference to the world.

"Many of us want to leave a mark that is more than our achievements on the pitch."

Manchester United star Mata set up the organisation at the start of the season - an initiative calling on footballers to give up one per cent of their income to good causes.

And following the likes of Giorgio Chiellini, Mats Hummels, Shinji Kagawa and Duncan Watmore joining up, Bruno has pledged his support.

Swansea defender Alfie Mawson and Bournemouth's Charlie Daniels became the first English players to sign up last month.

Mata set up the Common Goal charity earlier this year, aimed at helping under-privileged kids around the world, calling on players to make a donation.

The 29-year-old pledged one per cent of his wages - more than £70,000, or £1,400 weekly - to the charity.
