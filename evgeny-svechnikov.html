Evgeny Svechnikov – Early Life and Family

Evgeny Svechnikov was born on October 31, 1996, in Neftegorsk, Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk, Russia.

He was born to proud parents Igor Svechnikov and Elena Svechnikov. He has a younger brother: Andrei Svechnikov.

The Svechnikov family moved to Barnaul, Russia, following the 1995 Neftegorsk earthquake.

Ice hockey runs in the blood of the Svechnikov family. It’s not just Evgeny, but also Andrei, who plays professional ice hockey.

Their mother, Elena Svechnikov, has stood firm for her sons’ career since Day 1. She even moved to North America from Russia, even though she can speak only a few English words.

The mother of two believes that her presence would provide moral support to her sons during the game. Hence, she always tries to be present in her sons’ tournaments.

Evgeny is very close to his parents.

In fact, he sent his signing bonus back to his parents in Russia right after receiving it, with gratitude remarks to thank them for helping him get that far. His parents must be very happy and proud to have such a good loving boy.

Evgeny Svechnikov – Ice Hockey Career

Before NHL / AHL

Evgeny Svechnikov moved to Kazan, Russia, in 2010. He then began his junior career in 2012, playing for Bars and Irbis, junior teams of the AK Bars Kazan.

AK Bars Kazan, also known as Hockey Club AK Bars, is a Russian professional ice hockey team based in Kazan city.

Svechnikov made his professional debut with Ak Bars Kazan during the 2013–14 season when he was merely 16. 

In the second round, he was drafted 63rd in the 2013 Canadian Hockey League (CHL) Import Draft by the Cape Breton Screaming Eagles.

The Cape Breton Screaming Eagles are a junior ice hockey team in the Quebec Major Junior Ice Hockey League (QMJHL).

Svechnikov committed to the organization for the 2014–15 season. He was third in club scoring in his rookie season.

Similarly, he scored 32 goals and 46 assists in 55 games. Also, Svechnikov stood at the second position among QMJHL rookies.

Evgeny Svechnikov led his team in points-per-game at 1.42. In fact, he had an outstanding rookie season.

He then got named to the QMJHL All-Rookie Team. Also, he got the opportunity to be featured in the CHL/NHL Top Prospects Game.
