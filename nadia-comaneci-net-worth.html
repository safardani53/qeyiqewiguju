Nadia Comăneci: Professional Career

Nаdіа’s career started at a very young age. She was just when she first stepped into the gym for the artistic gymnastics team in her hometown.

Following training and continuous improvement of her skills, Nadia won the Romanian national junior championships. Similarly, as a senior, the young girl won the European championships in 1975 and the American cup in 1976.

Nadia thrilled the world at the 1976 Olympic games in Montreal, Canada, at 14. Hence, she became the first woman to score a perfect 10 in an Olympic gymnastics event.

Moreover, Comaneci won silver Domenech’s performance at the 1976 Olympics, redefined both her sport and the audience’s female athletes’ expectations.

Furthermore, the gymnast later led the nation’s gymnastic program to its first world championships. Finally, she retired from the competition in 1984 and worked as a coach for the Romanian team.

Thereafter, in 1999, she received a world award of the century after being elected athlete of the century. In addition, Nadia currently works as a coach at Bart Conner Gymnastics Academy.
