Age Gap &amp; Family Tree

Publish date: 2024-05-18

Jake Ferguson hails from a proud lineage of football enthusiasts, his elder brother Joe Ferguson making a mark as a safety for the Badgers.

Since Jake took his first steps, football has been an inseparable part of his life.

In the early years, he engaged in spirited matches with his older brother Joe, honing his skills in the makeshift arena of their basement.

The fierce connection between the Ferguson brothers extended to practice sessions, where they frequently squared off against each other.

Jake Ferguson, a standout American football tight end, plays for the Dallas Cowboys in the National Football League (NFL).

The Cowboys wisely selected him in the fourth round of the 2022 NFL Draft, recognizing his potential and skill set.

Before making waves in the NFL, he committed significantly to the University of Wisconsin.

Joe Ferguson: From Athlete to Acquisitions Manager

Jake Ferguson’s brother, Joe Ferguson, holds the position of Acquisitions Manager at The Bascom Group.

Commencing his journey with the company as an Acquisitions Analyst in July 2018, Joe’s dedication and proficiency led to successive promotions, culminating in his role as Acquisitions Manager.

Before his impactful career in acquisitions, Joe showcased his prowess as a student-athlete at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

There, he dedicated four years to academics and athletics starting in June 2013.

His commitment to excellence extended beyond the university, demonstrated through a notable four-month summer internship at MIG Commercial Real Estate, LLC, in May 2017.

The Ferguson Brothers’ Gridiron Journey

Joe Ferguson showcased his defensive prowess as a Badger safety from 2013 to 2018.

Their football lineage extends to their grandfather, Barry Alvarez, former college football coach at the University of Wisconsin–Madison.

Alvarez revealed a charming story from their childhood, recalling a playroom adorned with a blocking dummy—a symbol of their early introduction to the sport.

Per Alvarez, attending bowl games wasn’t just a pastime for the Ferguson brothers.

It was ingrained in their upbringing, a defining aspect of their identity.

Despite their shared heritage, Alvarez highlighted a key distinction between the two brothers, separated by a couple of years in age.

While Joe was concerned about preferential treatment due to their grandfather’s legacy, Jake embraced the family connection without hesitation.

Alvarez shared a humorous contrast in their interactions during football camp, where Joe wished for anonymity while Jake proudly proclaimed his relationship, showcasing their distinct personalities.

Amidst this football-rich backdrop, the inevitability of Jake Ferguson’s journey to collegiate football with Wisconsin became apparent.

The Ferguson Family Story

Born on January 18, 1999, Jake Ferguson is the son of Brad Ferguson and Chair Dawn Thomas.

Hailing from a family deeply rooted in football, Jake’s father, Brad, etched his name as a linebacker for Nebraska.

The illustrious lineage extends further with Jake’s grandfather, the esteemed Barry Alvarez, a revered figure enshrined in the College Football Hall of Fame.

Not just that, Barry Alvarez also served as an influential coach at the University of Wisconsin.

Beyond football, Jake’s mother, Chair Dawn Thomas, assumes a role of significance on the Fort Myers Beach Public Safety Committee.
