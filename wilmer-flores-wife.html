Wilmer Flores | Career

As per the sources, Wilmer Flores initially commenced showing interest in baseball at the age of six when he first picked up the bat. By thirteen, he had stood as a powerful athlete and even got formal tuition for the first time back then.

With his professional games, he first entered the minor league as an international free agent with the New York Mets. Back then, he became one of the Top 100 prospects by Baseball America. 

Right at the start, Wilmer bagged several titles and accolades under his name. Following it, he was later promoted to the Binghamton Mets of the Class AA Eastern League. In 2013, he moved to the major league and made his debut against the Colorado Rockies at Citi Field. 

The same year, he had his first major league hit, and by the following year, he had his first career grand slam. Hence, he became the first National League position player to hit a grand slam batting. 

In July 2015, Wilmer won many fans’ hearts at home and away games. The following year, he collected six hits in fun and became the first Major League Baseball player to accomplish the feat. 

Before leaving the Mets, Wilmer also broke franchise history for the most walk-off RBIs of any player. Not long after, he left the team to sign with the Arizona Diamondbacks for a single year. 

Right after 2019, he again moved to sign a deal with the San Francisco Giants, a two-year contract.
