William Howard Taft Biography

William Howard Taft was born on September 15, 1857, and died on March 8, 1930, at the age of 72. He was born in Cincinnati, Ohio, and he was a Virgo.

In terms of his family, Alphonso Taft and Louise Torrey were his parents. His father served as both a judge and an ambassador. His father was also the United States Secretary of War and Attorney General under President Ulysses S. Grant.

Taft, like his brothers, had four children: Charles Phelps Taft, Henry Waters Taft, Horace Dutton Taft, and Frances Louise Taft.

William Howard Taft Education

In terms of education, this individual attended Woodward High School. Later, in 1874, he enrolled in Yale College and graduated in 1878. Taft also attended Cincinnati Law School, where he earned a Bachelor of Laws in 1880.

William Howard Taft’s Death

William’s health was deteriorating by the time he was appointed Chief Justice in 1921. As a result, Taft meticulously planned a fitness regimen. In addition, this personality engaged in a weight loss program and hired British doctor N. E. Yorke-Davies as a dietary advisor.

Taft’s health deteriorated gradually during his nearly decade as Chief Justice. Taft took a vacation in Asheville, North Carolina, in January 1930. However, by the end of the month, this individual could barely speak and was experiencing hallucinations. On February 3, 1930, he resigned as Chief Justice.

When he returned to Washington after resigning, he barely had enough physical or emotional strength to sign a response to a letter of tribute from the eight associate justices.

On March 8, 1930, he died at his home in Washington, D.C. He was 72 years old at the time, and the cause of death was most likely heart disease, liver inflammation, and high blood pressure.

Career

Professionally, William Howard Taft is a politician by profession. He is also known as the 27th President of the United States, serving from 1909 to 1913.

Similarly, he served as the 10th Chief Justice of the United States from 1921 to 1930. This individual is the only person to have served as both president and chief justice.

President Warren G. Harding appointed this individual as Chief Justice in 1921. He held this position until one month before his death, on February 3, 1930.

Similarly, Taft worked for The Cincinnati Commercial newspaper while in law school and was assigned to cover the local courts. This person went to Columbus to take the bar exam shortly before graduating from law school and easily passed.

This individual was appointed a judge while still in his twenties after becoming a lawyer. Taft went on to become the solicitor general and a judge on the Sixth Circuit Court of Appeals, continuing his meteoric rise.

President William McKinley then appointed him as civilian governor of the Philippines in 1901. Similarly, Roosevelt appointed him Secretary of War in 1904. With Roosevelt’s assistance, this personality faced little opposition to the Republican presidential nomination in 1908.

In that November election, he easily defeated William Jennings Bryan for the presidency. Taft prioritized East Asian affairs over European affairs in the White House, and he frequently intervened to support or destabilize Latin American governments.

This individual returned to Yale as a professor after leaving office. He also kept up his political activism, working against war through the League to Enforce Peace. Furthermore, Harding appointed this individual as Chief Justice in 1921. Taft resigned in February 1930 due to ill health.

William Howard Taft’s Wife, Marriage

Helen Louise Taft (née Herron), also known as Nellie, was married to William Howard Taft. Helen and William married in 1886 and remained together until William’s death on March 8, 1930.

This lovely couple is the parents of three children: Robert Alphonso Taft Sr., Helen Herron Taft Manning, and Charles Phelps Taft II.

Nellie suffered a severe stroke early in Taft’s presidency, in May 1909. This paralyzed one arm and one leg and took away her ability to speak. William spent several hours a day caring for her and teaching her to speak again. Taft needed a year to complete this task.
