Pete Davidson lashes out at fan after he's slammed as 'racist' during comedy show in shocking moment

Publish date: 2024-05-17

PETE Davidson has hit back out at one of his fans, during an explosive comedy show that left no one laughing.

The funnyman was ripped as a "racist" during a performance Saturday night.

After an extended stay in rehab, Pete has returned to the stage, with a show Saturday night at 6 at the Wind Creek Event Center in Bethlehem, Pennsylvania.

According to a U.S. Sun reporter in the audience, Pete, along with his friends and fellow comedians Jon Stewart and John Mulaney, came out at the end of the show to answer questions from fans.

One woman shouted out that Pete was a "racist," to which Jon tried to defend explaining it was all just part of Pete's act.

But the accusation seemed to enrage the Saturday Night Live alum, who shouted out "you’re a c**t. Sit the f**k down!"

As the shocked audience looked on, Jon tried making light of the moment by joking "clean up in aisle 3."

However the moment had already been ruined, and the three comedians walked off the stage.

Eyewitnesses said that Pete's alleged "racism" came during a bit of his relating to home bedrooms.

"He was talking about his realtor, who is black," the source said. "And he said the realtor was showing him homes, and he called the big room the 'master bedroom'."

It's not clear what part of the joke the woman specifically objected to.

PETE'S WEEK

In January, the two were seen cuddling up together while enjoying a romantic date night in New York City.

REHAB

Earlier this summer, the comedian checked himself into rehab in June and resurfaced on social media in late July.

“Pete regularly goes to rehab for ‘tune ups’ and to take a mental break, so that’s what’s happening," a source told Page Six.

In 2017, when Pete was 23, his therapist diagnosed him with borderline personality disorder.

“I got diagnosed with BPD a few years ago, and I was always just so confused all the time, and just thought something was wrong, and didn’t know how to deal with it,” he said in a conversation with actress Glenn Close during Variety's Actors On Acting.
